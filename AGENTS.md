# House style

Every caption, title, dek, article, ranking, prediction and event line on MyKStars, plus the chrome around them (nav, taglines, meta/OG), follows four standing rules, going forward and whenever you edit existing copy. Full guide: [`docs/style-guide.md`](docs/style-guide.md).

1. **No em or en dashes** (`—` `–`). Recast with a comma, colon, period, or parentheses; for a title/brand separator use a middle dot (`%s · MyKStars`). Never fake a dash with a hyphen. The only exception is the decorative `aria-hidden` glyph in `src/components/RankingTable.tsx`. Run `npm run check:style` after editing any content file (`src/content/*.ts`) — it fails on any em/en dash, negation-reveal construction, or AI-tell phrase in content strings (comments are exempt).
2. **Italicize the names of works** — books, TV, films, albums, publications, games, stage works — with a markdown-style `*asterisk*` span (e.g. `"Cha Eun-woo for *W Korea*"`); songs and single episodes take `'quotes'`; people, groups, brands and credit bylines stay roman. `renderEmphasis()` / `stripEmphasis()` in `src/lib/text.tsx` handle display vs. plain-text contexts, and every title-bearing field is already routed through them.
3. **No negation-reveal constructions.** Never "It's not X, it's Y" negative parallelism or "Not X. Not Y. Just Z." stacked reveals; state the positive claim directly (a positive-first trailing contrast like "a floor, not a ceiling" is fine). Style-guide Rule 3.
4. **No AI-tell phrases.** "delve", "testament to", "ever-evolving", "remains to be seen", "only time will tell" and the rest of the banned list (canonical: `BANNED_PHRASES` in `scripts/check-style.ts`) never appear in content strings; name the specific fact instead of the hedge. Style-guide Rule 4.

# Fan Forecast questions

When updating the Fan Forecast (the `predictions` array in `src/content/predictions.ts`), follow the standing brief in [`docs/forecast-playbook.md`](docs/forecast-playbook.md): maximize engagement through stakes, identity, timing, and rivalry (never private lives), keep every question resolvable against a dated public source, and hold the portfolio at 8 to 16 strong open questions (the one-per-artist rule is retired). Verify each artist's current status by web search before writing (the site carries an explicit `NOW` date). Live votes are user data: published slugs are permanent, and retired questions get targeted deletes (`delete from votes where prediction_slug in (...)`), never a whole-table truncate. Question copy also follows the [house style](docs/style-guide.md) above: no em/en dashes, and italicize work titles with `*asterisks*`.

# Roster and embed freshness

MyKStars covers a curated, currently-iconic roster (the `artists` array in `src/content/profiles.ts`, where every profile carries `careerStage`, `coverageLevel`, `publicationState` and `lastVerified`; flipping `coverageLevel` to `"catalog"` benches a cold artist without deleting their pages). Video embeds are YouTube (TikTok reserved); Instagram and X are both retired, their handles kept in the content data as unrendered verification records (Instagram came back briefly in July 2026 as click-to-reveal photo embeds and was erased again on 2026-07-16, so no third-party photo embed exists). Photography comes from the permitted `MediaAsset` registry: openly licensed Wikimedia Commons files (`cc-by`, `cc-by-sa`, `public-domain`) or an agency press kit, re-hosted once to the Supabase media bucket with a rights record. Photos are searched under each artist's `koreanName` as well as their English name, because Korean outlets publish to YouTube under CC BY and those frames reach Commons under Korean-titled filenames, and because romanization drifts (Commons files Roh Yoon-seo under "Noh"); `node scripts/find-photos.mjs` runs that bilingual search and prints license-clean leads, which a human then confirms. NC and ND are both refused, ND because the site crops every photo `object-cover` into a fixed aspect box, which is a CC adaptation. Video clips carry a freshness obligation: every clip at most 180 days old, measured against the site clock `NOW`, always carrying the video's TRUE upload date (never relabel an old post as new); gallery- and Pulse-embedded media is archival and not age-gated, but must carry a true, non-future date. Two home rails split the clips by `genre`: In motion (music, the `yt()` factory, artist/label channels) and On air (comedy, variety and talk-show appearances, the `tv()` factory, the program's or broadcaster's official channel, and the roster artist must actually appear). On every refresh the On air rail re-picks, per featured artist, the most sensational current talk/variety appearance on YouTube, Korean or international, whichever is hotter. Before adding, cataloging, or reactivating any artist, or touching the `clips` array, follow the standing rules in [`docs/roster-playbook.md`](docs/roster-playbook.md): web-verify every artist's current status before writing anything about them, official channels only, new profiles enter through an owner-approved dossier and publish only with a permitted hero, and run the NOW-bump ritual (`src/content/now.ts`) with every refresh. Pre-debut (preview) profiles are guardrailed: agency-announced lineups only, activity-only coverage, never forecast subjects or ranking links. Run `npm run check:fresh` after editing content files; it fails on stale, undated, or future-dated embeds (a dated `Clip.evergreenUntil` is the only exemption). Run `npm run check:profiles` after touching `src/content/profiles.ts`; it guards slugs, stage/coverage/publication values, verification-date cadence, member relationships, the permitted-hero requirement for new profiles, and the pre-debut guardrails.

# Analysis articles

When writing or refreshing Analysis (the `articles` array in `src/content/articles.ts`), follow the standing brief in [`docs/analysis-playbook.md`](docs/analysis-playbook.md): every piece argues one falsifiable, clearly-sided thesis (insight, never recap), steelmans the strongest counter, backs load-bearing claims with the latest named-and-dated data (Circle Chart, Hanteo, Luminate, IFPI, KOCCA, company IR), and closes on a dated what-to-watch checkpoint. Analysis ships inside the monthly edition ([`docs/edition-playbook.md`](docs/edition-playbook.md)): at least 4 pieces per edition (more when the news cycle is heavy), mixing artist, company and industry scopes, balancing bullish and bearish calls, capping any one subject at two pieces, and never touching private lives or rumor (the forecast playbook's sensitivity rule applies). The byline is always exactly "MyKStars", never an invented reporter, and `Article.date` sits on or before the site clock `NOW`. Article copy follows the [house style](docs/style-guide.md) above, including the banned negation constructions and AI-tell phrases. Run `npm run check:style` and `npm run check:articles` after editing content files; the second fails on off-policy bylines, missing or future article dates, question-mark titles, dangling `related` slugs and duplicate slugs, and warns when the latest run comes in light.

# Monthly editions

Content publishes as one human-approved monthly edition per [`docs/edition-playbook.md`](docs/edition-playbook.md): 60 to 90 items mixing Pulse, official video, permitted photography and the other formats (substitution when photography is short: video, then Pulse), every active profile covered at least quarterly, deterministic ordering with diversity caps, and a Spotlight rotation that covers every active profile monthly. Editions are committed files under `src/content/editions/` and merge only after owner approval of a draft PR with a preview. The edition engine and `check:edition` land in wave 1b; until then the home page renders its standing hand-built plan.
